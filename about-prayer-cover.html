ABOUT PRAYER COVER
WHAT IS PRAYER COVER?

Prayer Cover is a framework that helps individuals and churches pray intentionally and consistently for the people around them.

Rather than praying generally for an entire city, Prayer Cover encourages Streetkeepers to choose and then focus on specific streets and neighborhoods, returning week after week to pray for the people who live and work there.

Every street represents people.

Families. Children. Neighbors. Business owners. Teachers. Workers.

People carrying burdens, facing decisions, celebrating victories, and searching for hope.

Prayer Cover helps turn a desire to pray for a community into a simple, concentrated, rhythm of prayer. Some people pray while walking. Some pray while driving. Some pray from their homes.

What matters is not the method, but the faithfulness.

Prayer Cover is not about ownership, territory, politics, or public recognition.

It is about prayer.

Prayer offered consistently.

Prayer offered specifically.

Prayer offered with the belief that God sees people, loves people, and is able to work in their lives in ways we often cannot see.

Our hope is simple:

That streets, neighborhoods, schools, churches, businesses, and communities would be quietly covered in prayer—one street at a time. And that this partnership with God will yield great results for the Kingdom of Heaven.

HOW WE STARTED

We had an idea to divide up our town by streets and sections and ask people to accept assignments, becoming a prayerful watchguard over their area. To know it, to take responsibility in prayer for those people—all those people who need Jesus in every way, every day.

First we thought maybe we would purchase a big map, copy it, cut it up, and let people take their sections home. Too much paper. Too many highlighters. Some ideas are just not meant to be.

Then came an election night. Watching the map change colors gave such a strong impression of what was actually happening. You could see it. It was tangible.

And we knew then that this was the way—an electronic map.

We knew, in our bones, that God was calling us to follow Him, to believe Him when He said that the harvest is plentiful and to ask Him to send forth laborers into His field.

So that is what we’re doing.
